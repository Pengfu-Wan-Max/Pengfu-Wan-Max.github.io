Research Overview

My research focuses on developing learning-based decision-making frameworks for complex, large-scale, and dynamic operations. I combine methods from reinforcement learning, stochastic optimization, and heuristic/metaheuristic algorithms to design intelligent decision support systems.

A central theme of my work is the integration of UAVs and other autonomous systems into emergency response and transportation networks, with attention to uncertainty, real-time coordination, and human–machine collaboration.

Current Topics

  • Reinforcement learning for routing and scheduling
    Designing deep RL architectures and training schemes to solve complex vehicle and UAV routing problems, including fleet sizing, fleet composition, and real-time collaborative routing across multiple agents.
  • Emergency medical delivery and disaster response
    Developing cooperative strategies that integrate UAVs and human couriers for emergency medical delivery, dynamic mass casualty management, and time-sensitive information collection after disasters.
  • Intelligent transportation and multi-UAV coordination
    Studying multi-UAV route planning, data collection, and scheduling in time-dependent networks, accounting for time-varying value of information and operational constraints.
  • Sustainability and wildlife operations research
    Exploring how learning-based decision tools can support sustainable operations and wildlife-related monitoring and management problems.

Selected Research Projects

Autonomous Sandy Beach Refuse Collection System with Unmanned Ground and Aerial Vehicles

Project member, 2024 – present

  • Evaluate and design schemes for an autonomous sandy beach refuse collection system.
  • Develop optimization models and algorithms for routing and coordination of unmanned ground and aerial vehicles.
  • Investigate robust and scalable strategies for cleaning operations under uncertain waste distributions.

Intelligent Emergency Response based on Cross-Domain Collaboration of Multiple Unmanned Systems (NSFC)

Project member, 2023 – present

  • Explore applications of multiple unmanned systems in diverse intelligent emergency response scenarios.
  • Study game-theoretic and cooperative mechanisms among heterogeneous unmanned systems.
  • Incorporate learning-based methods to enable adaptive, data-driven coordination strategies.
